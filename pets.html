Oreo

Oreo was adopted during the pandemic in 2020. How we got him is a bit different.
A housekeeper stole money from our family, then they bought multiple things, the dog, Oreo, as well.
When my parents checked the bought items from the stolen money, there was a dog in a cage, looking scared.

They couldn’t let Oreo be left alone and possibly end up in the wrong hands.
And so, when our parents got home, we were shocked that they brought a dog.
Oreo is a gift, and a family, that I truly cherish until now.



Waffle

Waffle was given to us for free. But it wasn’t easy, deciding if we should get another dog or not.
Our experience with Oreo during his young age has been fun, yet really exhausting.

We’ve realized that Oreo is pretty avoidant to other dogs, or is really shy.
And so, we thought getting another dog for Oreo to play with would be a great idea.
Thus, Waffle has became part of our family.

To this date, Oreo is still having trouble communicating with other dogs.
For some reason, Waffle is smaller, but more dominant towards Oreo.
Aside from this, there are still moments where both of them just plays for fun.
